Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers for students, instructors, and judges who want to understand how Skill Swap works.

What is Skill Swap in one sentence?

Skill Swap is a student-to-student platform where you exchange skills and time instead of paying money.

Who can join?

Any LAU student from any major can participate as long as they are willing to both offer and receive help.

Do I have to be an “expert” to offer a skill?

No. You just need to be comfortable enough with a topic to help someone who knows less than you (for example, if you already passed a course or use a tool regularly).

How do I actually contact other students?

When students fill the Join form, they share an Instagram handle or email. On the Skill Board, they can be contacted directly using that information to agree on a time and place.

Is this a real platform or just a prototype?

The version you see now is a front-end prototype created for the MGT401 Project Management course. It shows the user journey and interface. A back-end (database, Google Sheets, etc.) can be added in the next phase.

Does Skill Swap replace formal tutoring services?

No. It complements them by focusing on peer-to-peer support, practical tips, and course-specific experience from fellow students.

How is “fairness” maintained?

The concept is based on time credits: one hour of helping equals one learning hour you can use later. In a future version, a simple tracking system can monitor this.

Can this work at other universities?

Yes. The model is designed to be scalable. Only the branding and connection to student emails or IDs would need to be adjusted.

For the purposes of the MGT401 project, this FAQ demonstrates how we anticipate student questions and how the platform could be communicated if launched officially.
