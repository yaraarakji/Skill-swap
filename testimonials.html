What students could say about Skill Swap

These testimonials are written as realistic scenarios to show how Skill Swap can impact students from different majors.

“I finally understood statistics.”

“I was really stuck with my statistics course. Through Skill Swap, I found a student who already passed the class and they explained SPSS to me in a simple way. In return, I helped them with their English presentation.”

— Psychology student

“It feels fair and pressure-free.”

“I don’t like asking for ‘favors’ all the time. With Skill Swap, it feels balanced – I know that if someone helps me with AutoCAD now, I can help someone else with Canva or layouts later.”

— Architecture student

“I love that it costs 0$.”

“Tutoring can be expensive and not always adapted to our courses. Here we just help each other as students, and it’s free. It feels more like teamwork than like a transaction.”

— Business student

“It made me more confident in my skills.”

“I didn’t realize that my ‘small’ skills actually helped other people. Teaching someone how to organize a presentation or manage their time made me feel more confident too.”

— LAU student, year 2

“I met people from other majors.”

“Usually I only talk to people from my department. Through Skill Swap, I met students from psychology, design, and engineering. It changed how I see the campus community.”

— Engineering student

“This would work so well during finals.”

“Before exams, everyone is stressed. If Skill Swap becomes active, we could quickly find people who already passed specific courses and exchange last-minute tips.”

— LAU senior

Note: These testimonials are written as examples for the prototype and are not yet collected from real users. In a future version, this page could be filled with real feedback from students who participate in the Skill Swap pilot.
